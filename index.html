Introduction

My research background is in applied machine learning and computer vision. My current research concentrates on applying deep learning techniques to quantum simulations in material science, computational chemistry, and computational physics. Currently, I focus on using quantum machine learning in clean energy science, using graph neural networks (GNN) to predicate two-dimensional nanomaterial molecule properties, and applying self-supervised learning to study long-distance allosteric communication, the molecular mechanism of SARS-CoV-2 spike protein binding to human cell receptor ACE2, and protein folding paths.

Despite my busy teaching schedule, I have published over twenty papers in journals and international conference proceedings, including Journal of Chemical Theory and Computation, ACS LANGMUIR, ACS Journal of Physical Chemistry(b), ACM Multimedia Conference, IEEE Trans. on Multimedia, ACM Trans. on Multimedia Computing, Communications and Applications, and IEEE International Conference on Machine Learning and Applications.
